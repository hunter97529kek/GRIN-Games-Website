 27th Feb 2001

Ballistics is a Geforce 3 flagship!

After much secrecy we are pleased to reveal that GRIN has teamed up with the worlds No 1 3D card manufacturer in their strive for excellence. Being a high end game we chose to take the full potential of Nvidias new Geforce 3 card in use. Ballistics™ is a leading example of what great hardware and software can produce if teamed right. We have totally rewritten the Diesel Engine to take advantage of the Dx8 features supported by Nvidia™ such as pixel and vertex shaders. Ballistics™ is only the beginning in GRINs crusade for graphical extravaganza - but oh what a beginning. If you thought Ballistics™ looked ground breaking before, head for the screenshot section to see the extreme version from the new Diesel Engine fueled by the Geforce3 card. Enjoy!

- The GRIN team
 
 25th Feb 2001

GRIN appoints new Director of the Board

GRIN are pleased to announce that Mr. Tomas Franzén has been appointed Director of the Board.

Mr Franzén has joined GRIN to head the board of directors and guide the company in its strategy and business development. Mr Franzén is currently the CEO of the very successful high tech company AU System noted on the Swedish Stock Exchange.

"As CEO I'm very pleased to be receiving guidance and valuable help from Mr Franzén with his senior experience in business development and management of fast moving organizations such as GRIN. "

- Bo Andersson, CEO GRIN Inc

 10th Sep 2000 

- Welcome to the online Preimiere of GRIN!

GRIN is now ready to reveal its game and technology to the gaming community. We have stayed very quiet and in the dark for long. Our first appearance was the 3rd of September at ECTS 2000 stand NG 270. The purpose of this secrecy is of course to be able to develop our game and technology without giving away any half true promises to the gamers about what we will create and deliver. We now feel very comfortable with our setup; Ballistics™ the racing game of the future and the Diesel Engine™ the 3D technology behind the game. The visitors at ECTS were given the opportunity to check out Ballistics™ in action as well as play the thing. We’d like to congratulate Kevin Petersen from England who was the first guy on site to try Ballistics™ - going a full 1014 km/h down the city track.

  We trust the press community to serve you fans with screenshots but we will also post a few on site now and then as the project goes on.
