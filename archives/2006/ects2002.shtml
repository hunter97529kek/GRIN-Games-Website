 Inside Article

GRIN team ECTS report

The GRIN expedition is back from ECTS and boy, did we have a ball.

The GRIN stand was constantly filled with curious gamers and journalists that wanted to try what obviously was a gorgeuos game - the twelve flatscreens shared by four test stations and a huge plasma screen made sure no one made any mistakes about the visual strenght in the game.

Those who took the chance and played the game also found the controls being surprisingly easy to learn, even though they were put in charge of both a car and its free moving turret.

"Have you learnt to use the SHIFT key?" was the mantra of the show, constantly repeated by the GRIN staff on set.

These men in black, dressed in the latest piece of GRIN fashion - the "BANDITS - Phoenix Rising" T-shirt, were always ready to give a helping hand and give instruction whenever someone kept driving into the walls, give answers whenever someone wondered when the game is due release or give CPR whenever someone had a heart attack because of exposure to the shocking overall coolness of the game.

       
       

It was three days of hard work, promoting a game that had already been a year of hard work, but we are proud of the final product and it was absolutely great to see people enjoy it.

We would like to thank Mike Drummelsmith and Matrox for the support and hardware - the Parhelia Surround Gaming setups on the test stations sure generated a whole lot of attention and gave all the on site testers of BANDITS more visual glory than they could chew on.

Of course, we would also like to thank all of you who visited the stand; gamers, journalists and publishers - thanks for the your shown interest and feedback, but most of all; huge thanks for just giving us a chance to show you our game - we hope you like playing it as much as we liked making it!

...and don't forget to use the Shift key!
